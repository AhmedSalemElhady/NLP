0. On September 7 2001 about 1145 Alaska daylight time a wheel-equipped de Havilland DHC-3 airplane N254AW sustained substantial damage during a forced landing about 53 miles east of Cordova Alaska . During a telephone conversation with the National Transportation Safety Board investigator-in-charge on September 8 the airplane/lodge owner reported that while in level cruise flight the accident pilot noted a loud bang as the engine began to run rough and lose power . The airplane was registered to Thomas J Prijatel and operated by Alaska Wilderness Outfitting Company LLC Cordova Alaska .
