On June 25 2001 about 1930 Alaska daylight time a float-equipped Piper PA-18-150 airplane N7050 sustained substantial damage when it collided with tundra-covered terrain about 80 miles north-northwest of King Salmon Alaska . The airplane was being operated as a visual flight rules (VFR) cross-country government flight under Title 14 CFR Part 91 when the accident occurred . The airplane was operated by the State of Alaska Fish and Wildlife Service .
