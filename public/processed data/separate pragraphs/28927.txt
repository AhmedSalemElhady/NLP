While the solo student pilot of the Cessna 152 was on downwind leg for landing the air traffic controller directed the student's attention to a Boeing 757 aircraft that was preceding the student's aircraft for landing on the parallel runway . The student's flight instructor told the Safety Board that the student who had 136 hours of dual instruction and 20 hours of solo flight time had received instruction in wake turbulence avoidance at three points in ground instruction . Witnesses reported that as the student pilot was on final approach descending through approximately 100-foot altitude (agl) the aircraft abruptly rolled inverted and crashed short of the runway threshold .
