On March 28 2001 about 1300 Alaska standard time a wheel/ski equipped Cessna 180 airplane N91303 sustained substantial damage while landing at an off airport site located about 60 miles west of Iliamna Alaska . The right wing struck the surface of the frozen slough and sustained substantial damage to the leading edge and main spar . The airplane was being operated as a visual flight rules (VFR) personal flight under Title 14 CFR Part 91 when the accident occurred .
