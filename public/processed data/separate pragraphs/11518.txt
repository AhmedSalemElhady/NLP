A witness on the golf course stated he did not see any smoke coming from the airplane as it flew over his head and he stated the engine sounded like it was operating at full power just before the impact with the trees . Visual meteorological conditions prevailed at the time and no flight plan was filed . On April 9 2001 about 1208 eastern daylight time a Piper PA-46-500TP N262MM registered to LK Aero Corporation collided with trees shortly after takeoff from Vero Beach Municipal Airport Vero Beach Florida while on a Title 14 CFR Part 91 personal flight to Daytona Beach Florida .
