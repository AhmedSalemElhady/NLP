On September 2 2001 about 1430 Alaska daylight time a tundra tire-equipped Cessna 185F airplane N177BM sustained substantial damage while taxiing for takeoff at the King Salmon Airport King Salmon Alaska . During the turn the pilot said he heard a scraping sound and the control yoke wobbled . During a telephone conversation with the National Transportation Safety Board (NTSB) investigator-in-charge (IIC) on September 7th the pilot reported that he was preparing to depart and was back taxiing on runway 11 at King Salmon .
