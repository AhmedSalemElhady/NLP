According to a manufacturer's Safety Notice: 'No matter what causes the low rotor RPM the pilot must first roll on throttle and lower collective simultaneously to recover RPM before investigating the problem' . The pilot stated the helicopter was approximately 800 feet above ground level climbing 300 feet-per-minute at 60 knots airspeed when he heard the low rotor RPM warning horn and saw the corresponding low RPM warning light . The pilot reported there were no mechanical deficiencies prior to the low RPM warning and the engine ran to manufacturer's specification after the accident .
