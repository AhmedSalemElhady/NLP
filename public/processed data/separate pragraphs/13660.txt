On June 25 2001 about 1930 Alaska daylight time a float-equipped Piper PA-18-150 airplane N7050 sustained substantial damage when it collided with tundra-covered terrain about 80 miles north-northwest of King Salmon Alaska . During a telephone conversation with the National Transportation Safety Board investigator-in-charge on June 25 the supervisor for the State of Alaska's aircraft section reported that the accident airplane was one of two airplanes en route to Iliamna Alaska after a brief stop at a remote lake . The sole occupant an Alaska State Trooper who held a commercial pilot certificate but limited to private privileges for single engine sea operations was fatally injured .
