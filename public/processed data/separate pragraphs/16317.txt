During a telephone conversation with the National Transportation Safety Board investigator-in-charge on September 8 the airplane/lodge owner reported that while in level cruise flight the accident pilot noted a loud bang as the engine began to run rough and lose power . The flight originated about 1115 from the airplane owner's remote lodge located about 100 miles southeast of Cordova  and was en route to Cordova . On September 7 2001 about 1145 Alaska daylight time a wheel-equipped de Havilland DHC-3 airplane N254AW sustained substantial damage during a forced landing about 53 miles east of Cordova Alaska .
