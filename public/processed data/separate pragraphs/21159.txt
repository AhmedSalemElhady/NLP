Prior to departure icing advisories were issued to the pilot for his route of flight; temperatures aloft ranging from -6 to -8 degrees C The pilot advised the passenger of icing conditions prior to departure and reassured her they could deviate if those conditions were encountered . The ATC handbook stated that certain weather phenomenon may deserve weighted consideration when recommending an emergency airport; the pilot may elect to fly further to an airport with VFR conditions . Several minutes later Columbus Approach provided a vector back to the initial airport northeast followed shortly thereafter by a vector to a third airport about 125 miles southeast .
