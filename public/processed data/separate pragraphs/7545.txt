On September 7 2000 about 1320 Greenwich Mean Time a McDonnell-Douglas DC-10-30 N15069 operating as Continental Airlines flight 71 received minor damage when an uncontained engine failure occurred during a takeoff roll at Amsterdam Airport Schiphol (HAM) Schiphol The Netherlands . A locally-based General Electric engine representative stated that the engine was a General Electric CF6-50 . According to preliminary information provided by the Dutch Transport Safety Board the airplane aborted the takeoff due to an uncontained engine failure of the number 1 engine .
