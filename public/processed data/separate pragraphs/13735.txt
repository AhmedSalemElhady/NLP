On June 29 2001 about 1350 Alaska daylight time a wheel-equipped Piper PA-18-150 airplane N6881B sustained substantial damage during a forced landing at a private airstrip located about 2 miles north of Palmer Alaska . A detailed inspection of the airplane wreckage is pending . A witness to the accident reported to the Alaska State Troopers that just after the accident airplane departed from the 800-feet long airstrip the engine began to run rough and lose power .
