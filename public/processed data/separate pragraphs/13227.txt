During a telephone conversation with the National Transportation Safety Board (NTSB) investigator-in-charge (IIC) on May 31 2001 the owner/student pilot reported the flight was the second day of instruction conducted to gain additional experience operating on lakes and rivers in Alaska . The airplane suddenly veered to the right and the floats collided with the creek bank . The airplane was being operated as a visual flight rules (VFR) local area instructional flight when the accident occurred .
