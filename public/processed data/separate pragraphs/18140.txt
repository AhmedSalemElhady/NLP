On November 7 2001 approximately 2000 central standard time a Taylorcraft F19 N20002 piloted by a private pilot was substantially damaged during cruise flight when it struck a tree and subsequently impacted into a wooded area 9 miles west of Backus Minnesota . The personal flight was being conducted under the provisions of 14 CFR Part 91 without a flight plan . At 2019 the aviation routine weather report (METAR) for Park Rapids Minnesota 19 miles north of the accident site was ceiling 400 overcast 3 miles visibility and mist temperature 43 degrees Fahrenheit (F) dew point 42 degrees F winds 310 degrees at 11 knots and altimeter 3003 inches of Mercury .
