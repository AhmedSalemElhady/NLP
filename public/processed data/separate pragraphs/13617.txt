During a telephone conversation with the National Transportation Safety Board investigator-in-charge on June 25 the supervisor for the State of Alaska's aircraft section reported that the accident airplane was one of two airplanes en route to Iliamna Alaska after a brief stop at a remote lake . The airplane was being operated as a visual flight rules (VFR) cross-country government flight under Title 14 CFR Part 91 when the accident occurred . He added that both airplanes departed the remote lake about 1925 and no radio communication was received from the accident airplane after departure .
